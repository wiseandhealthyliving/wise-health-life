Preparation Guide

Infusion (Tea)

Best for: Leaves and flowers (Mint, Lemon Balm, Basil).

Pour hot water over herbs and steep covered for 5-10 minutes to keep essential oils from escaping.

Decoction

Best for: Roots and bark (Dandelion, Ginger, Cinnamon).

Simmer the herb in boiling water for 15-20 minutes to extract tougher compounds.

Poultice/Pack

Best for: External use (Castor Oil, Aloe, Turmeric).

Apply the herb directly to the skin or via a soaked cloth for localized relief.

Safety & Cautions

Pregnancy: Some powerful herbs like Blue Vervain or high doses of Nutmeg should be avoided. Always consult a midwife or doctor.
Blood Thinners: Herbs like Turmeric, Ginger, and Cayenne Pepper can naturally thin the blood. Be cautious if on medication like Warfarin.
Diabetes: Cinnamon and Aloe significantly lower blood sugar. Monitor levels closely to avoid hypoglycemia.
Potency: Essential oils and "Black" mint varieties are highly concentrated. Start with small "tester" doses.
